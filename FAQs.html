Mounjaro (tirzepatide) and Contraception FAQ

Important Contraception Warning

If you are taking Mounjaro (tirzepatide), you should use barrier contraception (e.g., condoms) for 4 weeks after starting or increasing your dose.

Important information about taking Mounjaro:

When to Inject

  • • Once weekly, same day each week
  • • Can be taken any time of day
  • • With or without food

Starting Dose

  • • Start with 2.5 mg once weekly
  • • Increase after 4 weeks if needed
  • • Maximum dose: 15 mg weekly

Missed Dose Instructions

  • If missed dose is within 4 days: Take as soon as possible
  • If more than 4 days: Skip the missed dose and take next dose on regular day
  • Do not take two doses within 72 hours

Before First Use

  • • Store in refrigerator (2°C to 8°C)
  • • Do not freeze
  • • Keep in original packaging to protect from light

After First Use

  • • Can be stored at room temperature up to 30°C
  • • Use within 21 days
  • • Protect from heat and sunlight

Important Storage Tips

  • Never use if solution is cloudy or contains particles
  • Keep out of reach of children
  • Do not use after expiration date
  • Never share your pen with others

GLP-1 agonists like Mounjaro (tirzepatide) can affect how your body absorbs oral contraceptives:

  • Delayed gastric emptying: Mounjaro slows down how quickly food and medications move through your stomach, which may affect the absorption of oral contraceptives.
  • Barrier contraception required: If you take Mounjaro, you should use barrier methods (like condoms) for 4 weeks after starting or increasing your dose.
  • Other GLP-1 medications: Wegovy and Ozempic (semaglutide) do not appear to affect oral contraceptive effectiveness in the same way.

What to do if you experience diarrhea or vomiting

These side effects can reduce pill effectiveness. Follow guidance for missed pills or consider switching to non-oral contraceptives.

Non-oral contraceptive methods are not affected by GLP-1 agonists like Mounjaro:

Intrauterine Devices (IUDs)

Both hormonal and copper IUDs remain fully effective.

Contraceptive Injections

Injectable contraceptives maintain their effectiveness.

Contraceptive Patches

Patches deliver hormones through the skin, bypassing digestive issues.

Contraceptive Implants

Implants remain fully effective with GLP-1 agonist use.

These methods are recommended for women taking Mounjaro who want reliable contraception without potential interaction concerns.

Important Warning

The effectiveness of emergency oral contraception is uncertain for women taking Mounjaro.

If you need emergency contraception while taking Mounjaro:

  • Copper IUD: This is the most reliable emergency contraception option for women taking Mounjaro.
  • Oral emergency contraception: May have reduced effectiveness due to delayed gastric emptying.
  • Seek immediate medical advice: Contact your healthcare provider as soon as possible for personalized guidance.

Recommended Action

If you require emergency contraception while taking Mounjaro, request a copper IUD insertion from your healthcare provider within 5 days of unprotected intercourse.

Important Notice

GLP-1 agonists should not be used during pregnancy.

If you are planning to become pregnant:

  • Washout period required: You should stop taking Mounjaro at least 1 month before trying to conceive.
  • Consult your healthcare provider: Discuss your pregnancy plans with your doctor before stopping any medication.
  • Alternative weight management: Your healthcare provider can suggest pregnancy-safe approaches to weight management.

Side effects of Mounjaro that may impact contraception effectiveness:

Nausea and Vomiting

If vomiting occurs within 3 hours of taking an oral contraceptive, the pill may not be absorbed properly.

Diarrhea

Severe diarrhea can reduce the absorption of oral contraceptives.

Delayed Gastric Emptying

May alter the absorption timing of oral medications, including contraceptives.

Appetite Changes

May lead to irregular pill-taking if medication is associated with meals.

What to do if you experience side effects

  • If you vomit within 3 hours of taking your pill, take another pill as soon as possible.
  • For severe diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours, follow missed pill guidance.
  • Consider switching to a non-oral contraceptive method if side effects are persistent.
  • Always consult your healthcare provider about managing side effects.

Guidance for switching between GLP-1 agonist medications:

From To Contraception Guidance
Wegovy/Ozempic (semaglutide) Mounjaro (tirzepatide) Use barrier contraception for 4 weeks after switching
Mounjaro (tirzepatide) Wegovy/Ozempic (semaglutide) No additional contraception precautions needed
Any GLP-1 agonist Non-GLP-1 medication Follow washout period guidance if planning pregnancy

Important Considerations

  • Always consult your healthcare provider before switching medications.
  • Dosing schedules may differ between medications.
  • Side effect profiles may vary between different GLP-1 agonists.

Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions About Mounjaro

What is Mounjaro and how does it work?

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) is a prescription medication used to improve blood sugar control in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus. It works by helping your body release the right amount of insulin when your blood sugar is high, slowing down food leaving your stomach, and helping stop your liver making sugar when it is not needed.

How should I store my Mounjaro pens?

Store your unused pens in the refrigerator between 2°C to 8°C. After first use, you can store the pen at room temperature up to 30°C for up to 21 days. Never freeze Mounjaro and protect it from heat and sunlight. Discard the pen if frozen or exposed to temperatures above 30°C.

What should I do if I miss a dose?

If you miss a dose and there are at least 4 days (96 hours) until your next scheduled dose, take the missed dose as soon as possible. If there are less than 4 days until your next scheduled dose, skip the missed dose and take your next dose on the regularly scheduled day.

Safety and Side Effects

What are the most common side effects of Mounjaro?

Common side effects include nausea, diarrhea, decreased appetite, vomiting, constipation, indigestion, and stomach pain. These side effects usually decrease over time. Contact your healthcare provider if side effects become severe or do not go away.

Can I take Mounjaro if I'm pregnant or planning to become pregnant?

Mounjaro should not be used during pregnancy. If you are planning to become pregnant, you should stop Mounjaro at least 1 month before trying to conceive. Discuss family planning with your healthcare provider to determine the best approach for your situation.

Are there any foods or drinks I should avoid while taking Mounjaro?

There are no specific food restrictions with Mounjaro. However, because it may affect how quickly your body absorbs food, you may need to adjust your eating habits. Follow your healthcare provider's dietary recommendations and monitor your body's response to different foods.

Usage and Administration

How do I properly inject Mounjaro?

Inject Mounjaro under the skin (subcutaneously) in your stomach area (abdomen), thigh, or upper arm. Change (rotate) your injection site with each injection. Clean the injection site with an alcohol swab before injecting. Follow the detailed instructions provided with your medication.

Can I take other medications with Mounjaro?

Tell your healthcare provider about all medications you take, including prescription and over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, and herbal supplements. Mounjaro may affect how other oral medicines work. Take oral medicines at least 30 minutes before injecting Mounjaro.

What should I do if I experience severe nausea or vomiting?

If you experience severe nausea or vomiting, contact your healthcare provider immediately. They may recommend anti-nausea medication or adjusting your dose. Stay hydrated and consider eating smaller, more frequent meals to help manage these symptoms.

Long-term Use and Monitoring

How long can I stay on Mounjaro?

The duration of Mounjaro treatment depends on your individual medical needs and response to the medication. Your healthcare provider will regularly evaluate your progress and determine how long you should continue treatment.

What monitoring is required while taking Mounjaro?

Your healthcare provider will monitor your blood sugar levels, kidney function, and other health parameters regularly. They may also check for signs of pancreatitis or other potential complications. Keep all scheduled appointments for proper monitoring.

Can I stop taking Mounjaro once I reach my treatment goals?

Do not stop taking Mounjaro without consulting your healthcare provider. Stopping treatment suddenly may affect your blood sugar control. Your provider will help you determine if and when it's appropriate to discontinue treatment.
